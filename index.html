What Little Lens is

Documentary filmmaking
is a craft worth keeping.

There is something irreplaceable about teaching a child to observe the world carefully, to find a story in it, and to tell that story honestly. That is what documentary filmmakers do.

Little Lens isn't an enrichment class. There are no assigned topics, no templates, no shortcut to a polished result. Kids choose their own subject, conduct real interviews, and make every creative decision themselves — from the first story pitch to the final cut.

The AI component

We don't teach kids to avoid AI.
We teach them to lead it.

AI is a working tool in documentary filmmaking. What we teach is discernment: the ability to know when AI is useful, when it's wrong, and when it simply cannot do the thing you need done.

AI proposes. You decide. You rewrite. You own it.

  • Use AIGenerate research questions — then find the real answers yourself
  • Use AISummarise a long article — but trace every fact to the original source
  • Use AISuggest a script structure — then rewrite every line in your own voice
  • Use AITranscribe interviews — saving hours, not replacing thinking
  • Never AIObserve, feel, go outside, or talk to a real person
  • Never AINotice the unscripted moment that changes everything

Who's behind Little Lens

Hi, I'm Joy.
I make documentaries.

I've spent over a decade telling real stories on film. My thesis documentary That's Wicked! won Best Documentary at the Singapore International Film Festival and screened at IDFA Amsterdam.

Little Lens was born from a simple belief — that every child has a story worth telling, and that learning to tell it honestly is one of the most powerful things we can teach them.

Questions worth asking

Before you join the waitlist.

Do kids need any prior experience?

None at all. Just curiosity and a willingness to try.

What equipment does my child need?

A smartphone is enough. For older groups, a laptop or tablet for editing.

My child is shy. Is this still a good fit?

Yes. Documentary shifts focus outward — onto a subject, a story. Many quieter kids find their voice this way.

How does the online format work?

Most sessions online. One on-location shoot day. Final screening for parents.

How small are the cohorts?

Maximum 5 kids per cohort. Every child gets individual attention.

My child is between age groups. Which workshop?

Use maturity as your guide. Email Joy for a quick conversation.
