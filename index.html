Hard Skills — things they can show
📷
Intentional filming

How to use a camera or phone deliberately — framing, light, and movement with purpose.

📝
Scriptwriting & structure

How to write a script and build a story with a clear beginning, middle, and end.

🎙️
Real interviewing

How to conduct an interview — and actually listen to what someone is saying.

Soft Skills — things parents notice
🧠
Critical thinking

Documentary projects push kids to seek truth, analyse perspectives, and synthesise information from real sources — not just accept what they're told.

🗣️
Communication & confidence

Presenting a film they made themselves does wonders for a shy child's confidence — they have to convey a message clearly, visually and verbally.

❤️
Empathy

Documentary is fundamentally about understanding someone else's story deeply enough to tell it honestly.

⏱️
Ownership & follow-through

Research, decision-making, iteration, time management — kids drive the whole project themselves.

The AI component

We don't teach kids
to avoid AI.
We teach them to lead it.

  • Use AI to generate research questions — then go find the real answers yourself
  • Use AI to summarise a long article — but trace every fact to the original source
  • Use AI to suggest a script structure — then rewrite every line in your own voice
  • Use AI to transcribe interviews — saving hours, not replacing thinking
  • Understand what AI genuinely cannot do: observe, feel, go outside, and talk to a real person
