The AI component

We don't teach kids
to avoid AI.
We teach them to lead it.

  • Use AI to generate research questions — then go find the real answers yourself
  • Use AI to summarise a long article — but trace every fact to the original source
  • Use AI to suggest a script structure — then rewrite every line in your own voice
  • Use AI to transcribe interviews — saving hours, not replacing thinking
  • Understand what AI genuinely cannot do: observe, feel, go outside, and talk to a real person

No two documentaries are the same

Documentary filmmaking isn't a formula. It's a form — and within that form, every filmmaker finds their own voice, their own obsessions, their own way of seeing the world. That's what we teach kids to discover.

Observational

The camera watches without interfering. No narration, no leading questions — just life as it happens. Some of the most powerful films ever made never say a word.

Participatory

The filmmaker steps in — asking, provoking, becoming part of the story. The process of finding the truth becomes the film itself.

Poetic

Mood, rhythm, and image over argument. Not every documentary needs to convince — some just need to make you feel something you can't quite name.

Investigative

A puzzle to be solved. Research, patience, and the willingness to follow a thread wherever it leads — even somewhere uncomfortable.

Performative

Deeply personal and unapologetically subjective. The filmmaker's own experience becomes the lens through which a bigger story is told.

Expository

A clear argument, a strong point of view, evidence marshalled to persuade. Classic, direct, and still one of the most powerful forms of documentary filmmaking.

At Little Lens, we don't tell kids which style to use. We show them all of them — and let them find the one that feels like theirs.
